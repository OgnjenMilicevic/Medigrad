# **Fisher's Exact Test**

[[Fisher's exact test]] tests the association between two [[categorical variable]]s and is **exact** rather than approximate, making it suitable for small samples.

## **When to Use It**

* The contingency table has small expected cell counts (e.g. fewer than 5 in some cells).
* The table is 2×2 (though extensions to larger tables exist).

## **Interpreting the Results**

* A significant [[p-value]] indicates that the two variables are not independent.
* No minimum sample size is required, unlike the [[chi-squared test]].
