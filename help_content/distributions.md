# **Distributions**

A distribution describes how the values of a variable are spread out. Understanding distributions is essential for choosing the right statistical test.

## **Key Concepts**

* **Shape** — Is the distribution symmetric, skewed to the left, or skewed to the right?
* **Center** — Where is the middle of the data? (see [[mean]], [[median]])
* **Spread** — How variable are the data? (see [[standard deviation]], [[IQR]])
* **Tails** — Are there extreme values or [[outlier]]s far from the center?

## **Common Distribution Shapes**

* **Symmetric / Bell-shaped** — Data are evenly distributed around the center. This is the hallmark of a [[normal distribution]].
* **Right-skewed (positive [[skewness]])** — The right tail is longer; most values are concentrated on the left.
* **Left-skewed (negative [[skewness]])** — The left tail is longer; most values are concentrated on the right.
* **Bimodal** — The distribution has two distinct peaks, which may suggest two underlying sub-groups.

## **Visualizing Distributions in Datagrad**

Use a [[histogram]], [[density plot]], [[violin plot]], [[box plot]], or [[QQ plot]] to inspect the shape of your data before running statistical tests.
