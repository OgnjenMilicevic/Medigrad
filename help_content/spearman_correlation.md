# **Spearman Correlation**

[[Spearman correlation]] measures the strength and direction of the **monotonic** relationship between two variables. It works on ranks rather than raw values.

## **How to Use**

1. Go to **Analysis > Correlation**.
2. Select **Spearman** as the method.

## **Interpreting the Results**

* The Spearman coefficient (**ρ**) ranges from -1 to +1, just like Pearson.
* A monotonic relationship means that as one variable increases, the other consistently increases (or consistently decreases), though not necessarily at a constant rate.

## **When to Use It**

* Your data are not normally distributed.
* The relationship is monotonic but not strictly linear.
* You have [[ordinal variable]]s.

## **Tips**

* Spearman is more robust to [[outlier]]s than [[Pearson correlation]] because it operates on ranks.
