# **Kruskal-Wallis Test**

The [[Kruskal-Wallis test]] is a [[non-parametric test]] that compares the distributions of **three or more** independent groups. It is the non-parametric alternative to [[ANOVA]].

## **When to Use It**

* The data are not normally distributed.
* You have ordinal outcome data.
* Group sizes are very unequal.

## **Interpreting the Results**

* The test statistic (H) is based on ranks.
* A significant [[p-value]] means at least one group's distribution differs from the others.
* Follow up with [[Dunn's test]] for pairwise comparisons.
