# **Descriptive Statistics**

[[Descriptive statistics]] are numerical summaries that describe the main features of a dataset without making inferences about a larger population.

## **Measures of Central Tendency**

* **[[Mean]]** — The arithmetic average. Sensitive to [[outlier]]s.
* **[[Median]]** — The middle value. Robust to outliers.
* **[[Mode]]** — The most frequent value.

## **Measures of Spread**

* **[[Standard deviation]]** — How far values typically fall from the mean.
* **[[Variance]]** — The square of the standard deviation.
* **[[IQR]]** — The range of the middle 50% of values (Q3 minus Q1).
* **Range** — The difference between the maximum and minimum values.

## **Measures of Shape**

* **[[Skewness]]** — Whether the distribution leans left or right.
* **[[Kurtosis]]** — How heavy the tails of the distribution are compared to a normal distribution.

## **In Datagrad**

Use **Description > Describe** to generate a full summary statistics report for all columns. For group comparisons, use **Description > Grouped Summary** to compute these statistics broken down by a [[categorical variable]].
